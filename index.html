Calculator Information

  1. Home value is the gross valuation of your residential property, you dont need to reduce it by the mortgage.
  2. Cash at bank equals to the saving your family has in place.
  3. Other investment includes but not limit to shares, managed funds, investment property and so on.
  4. Super balance refers to your superannuation only.
  5. Home mortgage is the outstanding borrowing on your own property.
  6. All the other debt refers to the current outstanding you have yet to repay to the lender.
  7. Financially dependent children are assumed to rely on your support till they turn 25.
  8. Family living expense is assumed to be supported by you till you turn 65.
  9. If the client selects to downsize the property in the event of death, the property would be sold at the price that the client indicated in the asset valuation, and the home mortgage will be fully repaid.
  10. If the new mortgage upon downsize plus the sale proceeds exceed the valuation of the new property that the spouse is going to buy, the surplus will be kept as an additional asset.
  11. Real rate of return and effective rate of return are used in the calculation.
  12. Asset excludes the home valuation since the family is assumed to continue living there and cannot cash out the equity.
  13. Scenario Two comes into account only when the client has a spouse who would like to move to a smaller property in case the insured event occurs.
